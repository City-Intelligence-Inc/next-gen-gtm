# Current GTM Landscape (2024-2026)

## The Traditional Model

Most B2B companies run GTM through a fragmented stack of 12-20 tools, organized around the CRM as the gravitational center.

### Team Structure (Traditional)

```
Marketing → SDR/BDR → AE → SE → CSM
    ↓          ↓        ↓       ↓
  MQLs       SQLs    Pipeline  NRR
```

Each team has its own tools, metrics, and incentives — creating **silos**.

### The Traditional Tech Stack

See: [[Traditional GTM Tech Stack]]

## What's Broken

See: [[GTM Pain Points]] for the full breakdown. The short version:

1. **Data silos** — 12-20 tools, no single source of truth
2. **Manual workflows** — SDRs spend hours researching, writing, updating CRM
3. **Tool sprawl** — $1,800/employee/year on overlapping SaaS
4. **Stale data** — CRM decays at ~30%/year
5. **Misaligned metrics** — Marketing measures MQLs, Sales measures SQLs
6. **Slow feedback loops** — Weeks between campaign → pipeline impact

## The Market in Numbers

- **15,384** distinct martech products on the 2025 Martech Map (39.2% CAGR since 2011)
- Average B2B company uses **12-20 GTM tools**
- CRM data decays at **~30% per year**
- A fully loaded SDR costs **$85K-$100K/year**
- An autonomous GTM system runs **$8.4K-$24K/year** for equivalent work
