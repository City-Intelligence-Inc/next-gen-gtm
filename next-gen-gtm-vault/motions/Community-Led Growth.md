# Community-Led Growth (CLG)

Users themselves become the primary drivers of product awareness, acquisition, and expansion through an engaged community.

## When CLG Works Best

- Developer tools, creative tools, platforms
- Products where users create and share work publicly
- Strong practitioner identity (designers, developers, data engineers)
- Products that benefit from templates, plugins, or extensions

## Key Mechanics

1. **Community platforms** — Slack, Discord, forums where users help each other
2. **User-generated content** — Templates, plugins, tutorials, YouTube videos, blog posts
3. **Ambassador programs** — Localized word-of-mouth (Notion Ambassadors, Figma Community Advocates)
4. **Events** — Conferences, meetups, hackathons (dbt Coalesce, Figma Config)
5. **Open source** — Code contributions, issues, PRs as community engagement

## How CLG Creates Pipeline

```
Community Activity → Brand Awareness → Product Trial → Adoption → Expansion
       ↑                                                              |
       └────────────── Advocacy (user becomes creator) ───────────────┘
```

## Measuring Community-Driven Pipeline

This is the hard part. Approaches:

- **Self-reported attribution** — "How did you hear about us?" → "community/friend/colleague"
- **Community-to-signup tracking** — Track community member → signup → paid conversion
- **Content attribution** — Track which community content drives signups
- **Influenced pipeline** — Accounts where contacts are community members
- **NPS/CSAT correlation** — Community members typically have higher satisfaction

## Strengths

- Compounds over time (content is evergreen, community grows)
- Extremely low CAC (organic, word-of-mouth)
- Builds moat through network effects and switching costs
- Users become product experts → reduces support burden
- **95% organic traffic** in best cases (Notion)

## Weaknesses

- Slow to build (months to years to reach critical mass)
- Hard to measure direct revenue impact
- Requires dedicated community team and investment
- Can't be faked — requires genuinely useful product
- Community sentiment can turn negative quickly

## CLG + PLG = Force Multiplier

CLG is rarely a standalone motion. It's most powerful when combined with [[Product-Led Growth]]:
- Community creates awareness → PLG converts to users → users create more community content
- Templates, plugins, and shared files serve as both community content AND product onboarding
