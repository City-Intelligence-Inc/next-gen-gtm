# Product-Led Sales (PLS)

The bridge between [[Product-Led Growth]]'s self-service efficiency and [[Sales-Led Growth]]'s enterprise deal sizes. Sales engages users who are *already using the product* and showing buying signals.

## The Core Idea

Instead of cold outreach to strangers, sales reps engage **warm leads who already know and use the product**. The product does the top-of-funnel work; sales does the expansion and enterprise conversion.

## When PLS Works Best

- PLG companies moving upmarket (SMB → mid-market → enterprise)
- Products with free tiers that naturally expand into teams/orgs
- When individual adoption leads to team/department buying decisions
- ACV $5K-$100K+ (too complex for pure self-serve, too small for pure enterprise sales)

## How PLS Works

```
Free User → Active User → Team Usage → PQL Trigger → Sales Engagement → Enterprise Deal
                                            ↓
                              Product-Qualified Lead (PQL)
```

### Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs)

PQLs replace MQLs. They're defined by **product usage signals** combined with **ICP fit**:

**Usage Signals:**
- Hitting usage thresholds (>10 active users, >X API calls)
- Inviting teammates
- Using enterprise features (SSO, admin controls)
- Spending >$X/month on self-serve plan
- Feature adoption velocity

**Fit Signals:**
- Company size, industry, tech stack
- Funding stage
- Known enterprise buyer

**Intent Signals:**
- Visiting pricing page
- Viewing enterprise plan docs
- Searching for competitors

### The Scoring Model

Modern PQL scoring is **transparent and configurable**, not black-box:

| Signal | Weight | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Active users in workspace | High | >10 users = enterprise potential |
| Usage growth rate | High | 3x usage in 30 days |
| Enterprise feature usage | High | SSO setup, admin panel visits |
| ICP fit score | Medium | Company >500 employees, Series B+ |
| Pricing page visits | Medium | 3+ visits in 7 days |
| Self-serve spend | Medium | >$100/month |

Tools: [[Pocus]], Toplyne, MadKudu, [[Common Room]]

## Segmentation

| Segment | Motion | Sales Involvement |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| SMB (<$5K ACV) | Pure PLG self-serve | None — automated upsells |
| Mid-market ($5K-$50K) | PLS hybrid | Inside sales, product-informed |
| Enterprise ($50K+) | Sales-led with PLG foundation | Dedicated AE + SE, product data informs strategy |

## Results

### Pocus + Asana
- **76%** of outbound pipeline fueled by Pocus-powered PQLs
- **45%** of quarterly pipeline from Pocus playbooks
- **33%** close rate on new pipeline (significantly above industry average)

### McKinsey Research
- Pure PLG rarely achieves outsize performance alone
- Companies implementing PLS most effectively enjoy sizeable boosts in both **revenue growth** and **valuation ratios**
- The hybrid motion outperforms either pure approach
