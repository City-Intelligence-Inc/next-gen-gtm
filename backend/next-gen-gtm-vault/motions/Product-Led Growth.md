# Product-Led Growth (PLG)

The product itself is the primary driver of customer acquisition, conversion, and expansion. Users discover, try, and adopt the product without sales involvement.

## When PLG Works Best

- Self-service products with **ACV under $10K**
- Mass-market appeal with low switching costs
- Products with short time-to-value (minutes to hours, not weeks)
- End-user is the buyer (not a committee)
- Network effects or viral loops are possible

## Key Mechanics

1. **Freemium tiers** — Free forever plan with usage limits (Notion, Figma, Slack)
2. **Free trials** — Time-limited full access (14-30 days)
3. **Viral loops** — Sharing, inviting, collaborating (Figma URL sharing, Calendly scheduling links)
4. **In-app upsells** — Usage-based triggers to upgrade
5. **Self-serve purchasing** — Credit card checkout, no sales call needed

## The PLG Flywheel

```
     ┌─────── Acquire ───────┐
     │                        │
  Advocate              Activate
     │                        │
     └─── Retain ← Expand ──┘
```

Each stage feeds the next. Happy users → advocates → new users.

## Team Structure

- **Growth Engineering** — Build growth features, optimize funnels
- **Product** — Core product experience, onboarding
- **Design** — Frictionless UX, self-serve flows
- **Data** — Instrumentation, funnel analytics, experimentation
- Minimal early sales (added later for enterprise — see [[Product-Led Sales]])

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description | Benchmark |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Time-to-value | How fast users get value | Minutes, not days |
| Activation rate | % of signups reaching "aha moment" | 20-40% |
| Free → Paid conversion | % of free users who pay | 2-5% (freemium), 10-25% (trial) |
| Viral coefficient | Users invited per user | >1.0 for viral growth |
| Net Revenue Retention | Revenue expansion in existing accounts | >120% |
| PQL conversion | Product-qualified leads closing | 25-50% |

## Strengths

- Low CAC (product is the acquisition channel)
- Faster time-to-revenue through frictionless adoption
- Compounds: each user can bring more users
- Product quality drives growth (forces you to build a great product)

## Weaknesses

- Hard to move upmarket (enterprise needs hand-holding)
- Free users cost money (infrastructure, support)
- Long payback if conversion rates are low
- McKinsey: pure PLG rarely achieves outsize performance alone

## The Evolution: PLG → PLS

Most successful PLG companies eventually add a sales layer:
- [[Product-Led Sales]] — Sales engages users showing buying signals
- **PQLs** (Product-Qualified Leads) replace MQLs
- Example: [[Case Study - Notion]] lets users adopt free, then deploys enterprise sales when teams spend >$100/month
