# Double-Loop Learning

**Chris Argyris' framework** for organizational learning. The difference between improving *what you do* and questioning *why you do it*.

## Single-Loop vs. Double-Loop

```
SINGLE LOOP:
  Strategy → Actions → Results → Adjust Actions (same strategy)
  "Our emails aren't working. Let's try different subject lines."

DOUBLE LOOP:
  Strategy → Actions → Results → Question Strategy → New Strategy
  "Why are we emailing cold? The data shows community signals convert 3x. Shift budget."
```

### GTM Examples

| Situation | Single-Loop Response | Double-Loop Response |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Low reply rate | Better subject lines, send times | "Is email the right channel for this segment?" |
| High churn at 3 months | Better onboarding sequence | "Are we selling to the wrong ICP?" |
| Long sales cycles | Better discovery scripts | "Should we switch from SLG to PLG?" |
| Low MQLs | More content, more ads | "Are MQLs even the right metric? Should we use PQLs?" |
| Lost deal to competitor | Better competitive battle cards | "What job is the buyer hiring for that we're not solving?" |

## Why Double-Loop Is Rare in GTM

1. **Metrics incentivize single-loop** — Teams optimize MQLs, not question whether MQLs matter
2. **Quarterly pressure** — No time to rethink strategy when the number is due
3. **Sunk cost fallacy** — "We invested $500K in this CRM implementation; we can't change now"
4. **Organizational inertia** — Changing strategy means admitting the current approach is wrong
5. **Data silos** — Without unified data, you can't see the patterns that suggest strategy change

## How to Build Double-Loop Into a GTM System

1. **Automated insight surfacing** — The system periodically analyzes cross-functional data and surfaces insights that challenge assumptions
2. **Quarterly assumption audits** — Explicitly list assumptions (ICP, channels, pricing) and check each against outcome data
3. **Win/loss analysis with teeth** — Not just "why did we lose?" but "should we have been in this deal at all?"
4. **Strategy experiments** — Reserve 10-20% of GTM effort for testing fundamentally different approaches (not just A/B tweaks)
5. **Cross-functional reviews** — Marketing, sales, and CS reviewing the same data together (not in silos)

## The Self-Improving Connection

The [[The Self-Improving GTM Engine|self-improving GTM engine]] needs both loops:
- **Single-loop (automated):** AI optimizes messaging, timing, channel selection continuously
- **Double-loop (surfaced to humans):** System flags when single-loop optimization plateaus and suggests strategic shifts

The machine handles single-loop. Humans handle double-loop. But the machine surfaces the data that makes double-loop possible.
