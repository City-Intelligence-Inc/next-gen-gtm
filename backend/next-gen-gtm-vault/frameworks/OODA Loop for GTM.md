# OODA Loop for GTM

**John Boyd's OODA Loop** (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) — originally a military decision-making framework — applied to Go-To-Market with a critical addition: **Learn**.

## The Framework

```
OBSERVE → ORIENT → DECIDE → ACT → LEARN → (repeat, smarter)
```

### Applied to GTM at Three Speeds

**Tactical OODA (minutes-hours):**

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Observe** | Detect a signal | Prospect visits pricing page 3x this week |
| **Orient** | Contextualize | They're an ICP match, showing intent |
| **Decide** | Select action | Trigger warm outbound sequence |
| **Act** | Execute | Send personalized email + Slack alert to AE |
| **Learn** | Track outcome | Did they reply? Book a meeting? Close? Update scoring model |

**Operational OODA (days-weeks):**

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Observe** | Analyze campaign data | Cold email campaign running for 2 weeks |
| **Orient** | Identify patterns | Subject line A gets 2x opens vs. B; vertical X converts 3x vs. Y |
| **Decide** | Adjust strategy | Double down on vertical X, kill subject line B |
| **Act** | Update workflows | Modify targeting and messaging |
| **Learn** | Measure improvement | Did overall pipeline increase? Update ICP model |

**Strategic OODA (months-quarters):**

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Observe** | Review quarterly data | Win rate dropping, churn increasing in mid-market |
| **Orient** | Question assumptions | Maybe our ICP has shifted. Maybe the product doesn't fit mid-market |
| **Decide** | Change strategy | Refocus on enterprise. Adjust GTM motion from PLG to PLS |
| **Act** | Restructure | Hire enterprise AEs, build enterprise features, adjust scoring |
| **Learn** | Track strategic shift | Is the new strategy working? [[Double-Loop Learning]] |

## Why "Learn" Is the Key Step

Most GTM teams do OODA but skip Learn:
- They run a campaign → check results → run the next campaign
- But they don't systematically feed outcomes back into models, scoring, or strategy
- Without Learn, you're doing the same thing with minor tweaks — not compounding improvement

**The self-improving GTM engine** automates the Learn step:
- Outcomes automatically feed back into scoring models
- Experiments auto-adjust based on statistical significance
- Strategic insights surface when patterns emerge across many OODA cycles

## OODA Speed as Competitive Advantage

Boyd's key insight: **the side that cycles through OODA faster wins.**

In GTM:
- If you detect a buying signal and respond in 5 minutes (fast OODA), you win
- If your competitor detects the same signal and responds in 5 days (slow OODA), they lose
- **Speed of the loop is the competitive advantage**, not the quality of any single step

This is why real-time [[Signal-Based Selling]] beats batch processing, and why [[AI Agents in GTM]] beat manual workflows.
