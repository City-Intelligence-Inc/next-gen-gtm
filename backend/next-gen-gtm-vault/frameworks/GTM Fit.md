# GTM Fit

**GTM Fit** is about ensuring your product is being sold and marketed in the right way for your prospects. It's [[Product-Market Fit]]'s sequential cousin — once you have PMF, GTM Fit determines how you get the product into the market efficiently and repeatably.

## The Progression

```
Problem-Solution Fit → Product-Market Fit → GTM Fit → Growth-Market Fit
     (Do you solve         (Does the market      (Can you sell     (Can you scale
      a real problem?)      want it?)              repeatably?)     efficiently?)
```

## Key Questions for GTM Fit

1. **Are you selling the right way to the right people?**
   - Is your [[Sales-Led Growth|SLG]]/[[Product-Led Growth|PLG]]/[[Community-Led Growth|CLG]] motion matched to your buyer?
   - Does your pricing model align with how customers get value?

2. **Do you have a repeatable and scalable sales process?**
   - Can a new hire follow the playbook and succeed?
   - Are win rates and cycle times consistent?

3. **Does your GTM motion match buyer purchasing behavior?**
   - Enterprise buyers expect demos and POCs → need sales-led
   - Individual users expect self-serve → need product-led
   - Developers expect free + open source → need community-led

4. **Is your cost of customer acquisition sustainable?**
   - CAC payback < 18 months
   - LTV:CAC > 3x
   - Unit economics work at current ACV

## Signs You Have GTM Fit

- Repeatable pipeline generation (not just founder-led deals)
- Consistent win rates across reps
- CAC payback < 18 months
- Clear ICP that you can target programmatically
- Customers stay and expand (NRR > 100%)

## Signs You Don't Have GTM Fit

- Only founders can close deals
- Every deal feels custom/different
- High churn in first 3-6 months
- CAC is rising while growth is flat
- You're winning deals but losing money on them

## GTM Fit ≠ Product-Market Fit

| PMF | GTM Fit |
|-----|---------|
| People want your product | People can find and buy your product |
| Validated by retention and engagement | Validated by repeatable revenue |
| "Pull" from the market | "Push" that scales |
| Can exist without GTM Fit | Cannot exist without PMF |

Many startups die with PMF but without GTM Fit — they have a product people want but can't figure out how to sell it repeatably.
