# The Bow-Tie Funnel

Developed by **Winning by Design**. Maps the full customer lifecycle across two symmetrically important sides — because in SaaS, the majority of LTV comes after the sale.

## The Model

```
         LEFT SIDE                    KNOT                   RIGHT SIDE
    (Pre-Sale Pipeline)           (Close/Won)          (Post-Sale Revenue)

    Awareness                                           Onboarding
        │                                                   │
    Education                                          Adoption
        │                                                   │
    Selection                        CLOSE              Retention
        │                          ╱      ╲                 │
    Evaluation                   ╱          ╲          Expansion
        │                      ╱              ╲             │
    Justification            ╱                  ╲      Advocacy
```

## Why It Matters

### The Traditional Funnel Is Half the Picture
- Traditional funnels stop at "close/won" — as if the job is done
- In SaaS, **60-80% of lifetime value** comes from renewals and expansion
- The bow-tie makes the right side **symmetrically important**

### Companies using the Bow-Tie grow **3x faster** (Winning by Design)

## Left Side: Pre-Sale

| Stage | What Happens | Key Metric |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Awareness** | Prospect knows you exist | Reach, impressions |
| **Education** | Prospect learns about your category/solution | Content engagement |
| **Selection** | Prospect shortlists you | Demo requests, trials |
| **Evaluation** | Prospect evaluates your product | POC completion, feature scoring |
| **Justification** | Prospect builds business case | ROI analysis, champion coaching |

Qualification frameworks like [[MEDDIC-MEDDPICC]] map to these stages.

## The Knot: Close/Won

The **moment of truth** — but also the **moment of highest risk**:
- Customer expectations are set during the sale
- If expectations ≠ reality, churn starts immediately
- The handoff from Sales → CS is where most companies drop the ball

## Right Side: Post-Sale

| Stage | What Happens | Key Metric |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Onboarding** | Customer gets set up and running | Time-to-value |
| **Adoption** | Customer achieves initial outcomes | Feature adoption, DAU/MAU |
| **Retention** | Customer renews | Gross revenue retention, NRR |
| **Expansion** | Customer buys more (upsell, cross-sell, seats) | Expansion revenue, NRR |
| **Advocacy** | Customer recommends you | NPS, referrals, case studies |

## Metrics at Each Stage

### Left Side Metrics
- **Pipeline coverage:** 3-4x of quota
- **Conversion rates:** Stage-to-stage progression
- **Velocity:** Time per stage
- **Win rate:** Closed/won ÷ opportunities created

### Right Side Metrics
- **Time-to-value:** How fast customers get value
- **Adoption rate:** % of features/seats being used
- **Gross retention:** % of revenue retained (excluding expansion)
- **Net retention (NRR):** % of revenue retained including expansion
- **Expansion rate:** Revenue growth within existing accounts
- **NPS/CSAT:** Customer satisfaction

## The Key Insight: Right Side > Left Side

For mature SaaS companies:
- [[Case Study - Datadog]]: **75%** of growth from expansion (right side)
- ~120% NRR means existing customers grow revenue without any new sales
- The best predictor of revenue is **retention + expansion**, not new logo acquisition

## How to Apply

1. **Map your current funnel** — Where do customers drop off?
2. **Instrument both sides** — Most companies only measure the left side
3. **Invest symmetrically** — CS and expansion deserve as much attention as acquisition
4. **Align incentives** — Sales should care about activation; CS should care about expansion
5. **Build feedback loops** — Right-side signals (health scores, NPS) should inform left-side targeting
