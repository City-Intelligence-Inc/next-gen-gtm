# Pocus

**Category:** Product-led sales platform
**Position:** The primary tool for building and operationalizing [[Product-Led Sales]]
**Website:** pocus.com

## What It Does

Pocus combines **product usage signals** with **ICP fit** and **intent data** to generate Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs) and surface them to sales teams with full context.

## Core Capabilities

### PQL Scoring
- No-code scoring models combining:
  - Product usage (active users, feature adoption, usage spikes)
  - ICP fit (company size, industry, tech stack)
  - Intent signals (pricing page visits, competitor research)
- Transparent scoring — teams can see *why* a lead scored high
- Configurable thresholds and weights

### Playbook Builder
- Pre-built and custom playbooks for common PLS scenarios:
  - "Team growing fast" → alert AE
  - "Enterprise feature usage" → trigger enterprise outreach
  - "Usage declining" → alert CSM
- Automated routing to the right rep

### Rep Workspace
- Unified view of product usage + CRM data + signals
- "Here's why you should reach out to this account NOW"
- Recommended actions and talking points

## Results

### Asana (Flagship Case Study)
- **76%** of outbound pipeline fueled by Pocus PQLs
- **45%** of quarterly pipeline from Pocus playbooks
- **33%** close rate on new pipeline

### General Impact
- Companies using PLS with Pocus report higher conversion rates than traditional MQL-based approaches
- Sales reps spend time on accounts that actually want to buy (not cold lists)

## Who Uses It

- [[Product-Led Sales]] teams at PLG companies moving upmarket
- Revenue teams at companies like Asana, Miro, Loom
- Used alongside CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot) — not a replacement
